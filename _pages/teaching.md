I view teaching as the cultivation of curiosity, critical thinking, and problem-solving abilities. My goal is to create a supportive learning environment where students can actively engage with abstract concepts and build confidence in their reasoning.

## Teaching Philosophy: The "Macro-Micro" Goal
Inspired by my research in numerical partial differential equations, I apply a "macro-micro" perspective to teaching: helping students grasp the "big picture" while strengthening their understanding through steady, detailed learning in daily coursework. 

I emphasize three core elements in my classroom:
1. **Preparation and Pedagogical Clarity:** I tailor practice problems, anticipate difficulties, and connect abstract ideas to real-world STEM disciplines. 
2. **Feedback-Oriented Course Development:** Drawing on my background in psychology, I utilize continuous mutual feedback via surveys, detailed grading notes, and open communication channels (like Piazza) to refine my methods.
3. **Inclusive and Welcoming Environment:** I am committed to maintaining an empathetic classroom space that respects all backgrounds and learning styles, mindful of the impact of language and tone in mathematics.
