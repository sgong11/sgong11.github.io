My research goal is to leverage high-performance computing, machine-learning techniques, and numerical analysis to develop structure-preserving, high-fidelity solvers for complex multiscale physical phenomena. 

## Current Work at Michigan State University
Funded by the CHARMNET Center, my recent work focuses on developing high-efficiency schemes for plasma physics:
* **Adaptive-Rank Methods for Wigner-Poisson Systems:** Developing mass-conserving, structure-preserving, adaptive-rank solvers to overcome the curse of dimensionality in high-dimensional PDEs.
* **Kernel-based Operators (MOLT):** Developing unconditionally stable, high-order kernel-based methods for multiscale nonlinear PDEs involving stiff and non-stiff terms. 
* **High Order SSP Multi-derivative Schemes:** Proposing unconditionally strong-stability-preserving implicit schemes for kinetic equations.

## Prior Work at Brown University
My doctoral research centered on the combination of Finite Element Exterior Calculus (FEEC) and spline theories to address:
* **Maxwell-Eigenvalue Problems:** Providing the first 3D analysis for the convergence of Lagrange elements on Worsey-Farin (WF) splits.
* **Elasticity Elements:** Constructing the first exact symmetry elasticity elements in 3D without requiring vertex degrees of freedom.
* **Shape Regularity:** Providing the rigorous proof for the shape regularity of WF splits.
